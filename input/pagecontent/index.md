# HL7 European EHDS Implementation Guide

## Introduction and Purpose

This **Implementation Guide (IG)** provides a **European FHIR-based foundation** to support the **European Health Data Space (EHDS)**. It brings together selected **HL7 Europe implementation assets** in a single guide to support **consistent**, **interoperable**, and **reusable health data exchange** across European contexts.

Its purpose is to **simplify the adoption of FHIR** for **EHDS-related use cases** by providing a **coherent set of conformance resources** that can support **harmonized implementation**, **cross-border interoperability**, and more consistent use of health data across systems and jurisdictions.

This guide currently includes implementation assets derived from the following **HL7 Europe guides**:

- **HL7 Europe Base and Core FHIR IG** — Foundational profiles and **reusable conformance resources** for core FHIR implementation in the European context.
- **HL7 Europe Laboratory Report IG** — Profiles and related assets for **laboratory reporting** and **diagnostics**.
- **HL7 Europe Imaging Report R5 IG** — Profiles and related assets for **imaging studies**, **imaging reports**, and **associated clinical information**.

## Audience

This guide is intended for **implementation guide authors, system vendors, integrators, architects, and other stakeholders** involved in designing, implementing, or maintaining **FHIR-based data exchange** in support of **EHDS-related use cases**. Readers are expected to have at least a **basic familiarity with FHIR** and to refer to the **core FHIR specification** where additional background is needed.

**Implementation, testing, and feedback** on this guide are encouraged as part of its continued development.

## Scope and Key Content

This guide provides a **reusable set of European conformance assets** intended to support **EHDS-aligned implementations**, including:

- **Base and Core Profiles:** Core FHIR resource profiles and related conformance resources adapted for **European healthcare interoperability needs**.
- **Laboratory Reporting:** Profiles and supporting artifacts for **laboratory observations**, **reports**, and **diagnostics workflows**.
- **Imaging Reporting:** Profiles and supporting artifacts for **imaging studies**, **imaging reports**, and **related clinical data exchange**.
- **Terminology Resources:** Reusable terminology assets, including **ValueSets** and other conformance resources needed for **consistent data representation**.
- **Implementation Foundation:** A **common European baseline** that can support **cross-border exchange** and serve as a foundation for more specific **national or domain-level implementation guides**.

These artifacts are intended to support **reuse and harmonization** across **EHDS-related implementations** while allowing further specialization where required by specific use cases or jurisdictions.

## Why Use This Guide?

By adopting these profiles and guidance, implementers can support a standardized approach to:

- **Interoperability:** Establish a **shared European baseline** for consistent and **semantically interoperable health data exchange** across **systems**, **organizations**, and **national boundaries**.
- **Data Quality:** Promote more consistent **modelling**, **terminology use**, and **conformance expectations** across EHDS-related implementations.
- **Clinical Utility:** Support **reusable and scalable implementation** of **cross-border clinical data exchange** by making important health information available in **structured and comparable form**.
